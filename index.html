A 20-Year-Old's Journey into AI: Animal Detection That Actually Works

By Massed Compute | June 2025

Sunny Smith didn’t like deer eating his carefully planted garden. Instead of turning to traditional fencing or repellents, he turned to tech—and gave 20-year-old developer Tyler Sackel a challenge: build something smarter.

The goal? Create a hands-on AI project that would deter animals, provide real-world experience in deploying models, and demonstrate the power of Massed Compute’s hardware. What followed was a collaboration between curiosity and compute.

At just 20 years old, I decided to build something that could combine my love for AI and my annoyance with deer trampling through the backyard. What started as a YOLOv8 experiment has now evolved into a full-fledged, real-time animal detection and deterrent system—hosted in the cloud and running on an RTX A6000.

Why I Built This

It all started with a problem: animals—mostly deer—kept destroying gardens and sneaking past fences. Motion lights weren’t cutting it, and I wondered if I could use object detection models to respond more intelligently.

How It Works

The system uses a Reolink infrared camera, detects motion using YOLO, and classifies the animal in real time. If a deer is detected, it triggers lights, sound, and even a sprinkler system—all controlled via smart relays. All of this runs on a Massed Compute-hosted server with GPU acceleration.

What’s Under the Hood

  • YOLOv8 for fast and accurate animal detection
  • Python + OpenCV to handle camera streams
  • Flask web app to review footage and logs
  • Smart device integration via Shelly relays
  • All hosted on Massed Compute’s RTX A6000 platform

What’s Next?

I’m expanding this to support multiple cameras, alert customization (email/SMS), and zone-based detection. Ultimately, I’d love to turn this into an installable smart system for homeowners and farms.
